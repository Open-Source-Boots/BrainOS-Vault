INSIGHTS & PATTERNS
Brayden's strongest asset in the CommonGrounds/Skool play is not technical skill — it's lived context. The DSP/caregiver audience is underserved precisely because most AI tool builders have never worked in the field. That gap is the moat.

The novelty loop risk is real here: Skool + POD + Whisper + YouTube + custom apps surfaced in one conversation. The architecture is coherent but the gate is singular — Whisper running locally, one afternoon. Everything else waits. Flag if new directions open before that gate is cleared.

The "learn in public" frame (YouTube) is structurally better for Brayden than a polished launch frame — it matches his burst pattern, lowers the first-step cost, and generates content as a byproduct of doing the actual work.
