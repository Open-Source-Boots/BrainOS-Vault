| Reason | Natural Response |
|--------|-----------------|
| "You're working full-time, not priority" | "I understand. My income is tight and I have no savings for a $2,600 tuition. What would I need to document to qualify — income level, underemployment, barriers?" |
| "We can only cover part of it" | "What's the maximum you can cover? And do you usually coordinate with Peaslee's RISE or local grants so we can stack and close the gap?" |
| "You seem self-sufficient" | "Being employed doesn't mean I can afford this. This training is how I move from a capped wage into a trade with real long-term earning. That's exactly what WIOA is designed to support." |
| "Program isn't on our list" | "Peaslee lists your office as a grant partner on their website. Is there a way to get this course approved, or is it already in your system under another name?" |
| "Our funds are committed" | "Is there a waitlist, or a better time to apply when new funds come in? Could I get pre-approved so I'm first in line?" |
| Flat "no" | "What specifically is the barrier — my income, the program, or funding levels? Is there anything I can document or do to change that? What would you do in my situation?" |

**Closing move on any pushback:** "Given your experience, if you were in my shoes and wanted this electrician training with minimal out-of-pocket — what are the top 2–3 steps you'd take?"
