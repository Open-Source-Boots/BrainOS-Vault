## 📺 The Channel — What It Actually Is

### Working Thesis (BrayDonDon)
> "This channel exists to figure out what it feels like to be young and trying to understand how the world actually works — when the systems you live inside keep changing the rules."

This is not a finance channel. Finance, history, psychology, urban design, and politics are all valid lenses — the thread that makes them coherent is **Brayden's perspective and lived experience**, not a topic category.

### One-Line Channel Pitch (for AI context)
A learning-in-public channel where a working-class 24-year-old from Kansas documents what it actually feels like to figure out how money, systems, and adult life work — told through real stories, behavioral psychology, and financial history, with the honest perspective of someone who learned the expensive way.

## 🧭 The Core Creative Angle: "The Gap"

The most resonant, repeatable frame for this channel is:

> **The gap between how systems are described and how they actually function.**

Every institution, market, policy, or cultural norm was built by someone, for reasons that were rarely fully explained — and those reasons often diverge from the stated purpose over time. Content that exposes this gap is:
- Universally relatable (everyone has felt it)
- Intellectually honest (no conspiracy needed — just observation)
- Emotionally engaging (it validates the viewer's quiet suspicion that something is off)
- Endlessly renewable as a content engine

This is the implicit question running beneath every video: *"Who built this, and why doesn't it work the way they said it would?"*

## 🎭 Visual Style: "Documentary Punk"

The target aesthetic is the intersection of:
- **Mini-documentary** — high-contrast, cinematic grading, deliberate pacing, real archival material
- **Music video** — kinetic energy, fast cuts during rising action, bold on-screen type as primary visual element
- **Personal zine** — hand-made quality, imperfect authenticity, not corporate polish

### Specific Visual Principles
- **Bold kinetic text is the primary visual** — numbers, names, dates slam onto screen in sync with narration; this is more powerful than stock footage in most cases
- **Historical archival images > stock actors** — real photos of Nick Leeson in handcuffs beat any stock clip of "guilty businessman"
- **Ken Burns effect on stills** — slow zoom/pan on AI-generated period images creates cinematic feel from static assets
- **Strategic silence** — hold a shot at the climax; let the number or image breathe for one beat before continuing
- **Consistent character cards** — every person introduced gets a clean title card (name, role, year); this becomes a visual signature
- **High-contrast, desaturated color grade** — serious, documentary feel; available as a LUT preset in CapCut

## 🪝 The Short-Form Script Template

Every Short follows this emotional architecture. The formula is invisible to the viewer — it maps to how human attention actually works.

| Beat | Duration | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | 0–4 sec | One sentence. Creates a question the viewer *must* answer. Shock, tension, or paradox. |
| **Setup** | 4–15 sec | Who, what, when. Minimum viable context. |
| **Rising Action** | 15–35 sec | Stakes escalate. Something is about to go wrong. |
| **Climax** | 35–48 sec | The specific moment everything changes. The twist, reveal, or collapse. |
| **Resolution + Lesson** | 48–55 sec | What happened after + one principle the viewer takes away. |
| **CTA** | 55–60 sec | One sentence. Subscribe + comment prompt. |

**Target word count:** 150–175 words. At Scenith (~0.95x speed with pauses) = 55–62 seconds.

**Rule:** The hook should create a *question* the viewer needs answered, not just a surprising fact. The difference is between passive interest and active engagement.

## 👥 Target Audience (Refined)

**Primary:** People in their late teens to early 30s who feel like the future they were promised never arrived — but who don't have language yet for *why* things feel off.

**Key insight:** This audience is NOT primarily looking for finance content. They are looking for someone who makes the world feel legible. Finance, history, and psychology are the vehicles — recognition and clarity are the product.

**Demographic note:** Traditional finance content skews heavily male in its vocabulary (alpha, beat the market, outperform) and frames money as competition. Brayden's approach — money as psychology, systems, history, and lived experience — naturally reaches a broader and less-served audience without dumbing anything down.

## 🔁 The Consistency Engine

The channel will never run out of content because it is built on a **worldview**, not a topic list.

**Daily capture rule:** Keep a running note (phone Notes app or Obsidian mobile) for any moment of confusion, surprise, or "that doesn't match the explanation I was given." One sentence. Don't develop it. This becomes the content backlog.

**The learning-in-public model:** Brayden's genuine curiosity IS the content. The moment he becomes "the expert delivering knowledge" rather than "the person figuring it out," the channel loses its most distinctive quality. Maintain this even as knowledge deepens.

## 🛠️ Production System (Minimum Viable)

| Phase | Task | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| Idea capture | Log moments of confusion/surprise | Phone Notes → Obsidian |
| Research + scripting | Turn idea into 4-line pre-script, then full script | Perplexity (this) |
| Voiceover | Record AI voice from script | Scenith |
| Asset gathering | Archival images (Wikipedia Commons), AI stills (Bing Creator/Ideogram), stock (Pixabay) | Free tools |
| Edit | Layer footage + kinetic text + captions | CapCut (Shorts) / DaVinci Resolve (long-form) |
| Publish | YouTube Shorts → community tab → long-form | YouTube Studio |

**Rule:** AI handles research, drafts, clip ideas. Brayden handles judgment, selection, final edit decisions, and publishing. The system reduces friction — it does not replace human taste.
