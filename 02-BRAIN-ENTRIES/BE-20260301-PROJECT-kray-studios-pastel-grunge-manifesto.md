## INSIGHTS & PATTERNS

- Brayden generates documentation and playbooks faster than the AI agent can execute them — bottleneck is AI understanding, not planning.
- Browser-control AI works best with atomic, imperative instructions ("click X, type Y, save") — fails with design-philosophy language.
- The gap between "what Brayden wrote down" and "what the AI is actually building" is a recurring structural problem across multiple sessions.
- Files in Space become stale quickly when the project pivots; Brayden explicitly flagged this — systematic versioning/dating of brand files is needed.
- ADHD pattern: multi-phase planning sessions that produce excellent reference material but don't immediately translate to live execution.
- Chunking the manifesto into named micro-functions (e.g., build_homepage_v1()) is a strong pattern for AI task-handoff — makes tasks concrete and bounded.
- Design philosophy should be translated into yes/no checklists and named action sequences before handing to any AI agent.
