## INSIGHTS & PATTERNS

- Brayden's housing, income, and employment are legally fused into a single 
  instrument controlled by GoodLife — this is the most structurally 
  significant risk in the entire situation
- The NDA embedded in a housing contract is unusual and specifically designed 
  to suppress PFT-to-PFT wage comparison — exactly the behavior NLRA 
  Section 7 is designed to protect (in the employee context)
- Strong guardian relationships with persons served are not soft goodwill — 
  they are real structural leverage against employer retaliation
- HCBS federal protections for the person served can function as a shield 
  for the provider household, even though that's not their primary purpose
- The "two-hat" problem (W2 employee + contractor simultaneously) creates 
  asymmetric legal exposure that needs to be resolved before any public 
  organizing action
- Brayden tends to assume third-party arrangements where the employer has 
  actually retained direct control — worth auditing other assumptions 
  through the same document-first approach
