## Why Nothing Was Acted On

Honest answer captured directly:

> "I was nervous, didn't feel like the website, the service, or the value were
> where they needed to be at to get a customer to confidently purchase from us.
> Then had life come in the way — work, sleep, schedules."

This is important to encode — not as a failure note, but as a pattern to design around.
The barrier was not laziness. It was:
1. **Confidence gap** — the offer/site didn't feel ready enough to back with conviction
2. **Life compression** — Thu–Sat 7a–7p shifts leave Sun–Wed as the only real work window,
   and those get eaten by recovery, family, and basic life maintenance

**Design implication:** Future action plans must account for both. The confidence gap
requires a lower-stakes first action (audit offer, not sales pitch). The time gap
requires pre-loaded, low-friction tasks that can be done in 30-minute blocks.
