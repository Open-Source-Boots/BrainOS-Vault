## 🎬 Video & Scripting System

> Note: A Notion workspace was built during this thread but was subsequently deleted by Brayden. The system below is extracted as a standalone framework to be rebuilt in Obsidian or another chosen tool.

### The Four-Line Pre-Script (Core Framework)
Before writing any video — short or long — answer these four lines. If you cannot fill all four, the idea is not ready.

1. **Hook** — What one moment, image, or question earns the first 5 seconds?
2. **Personal Angle** — What real memory or feeling connects you to this topic?
3. **System Angle** — What bigger pattern or structure is underneath it?
4. **Takeaway** — What does the viewer walk away with — a feeling, a reframe, a question?

### Short-Form Script Structure (Clips / Shorts)
- **Hook (0–5 sec):** Drop into the moment. No intro. No setup.
- **Setup (5–30 sec):** Personal angle. Why does this matter? Keep it specific.
- **Escalation:** Widen to the system. What is actually going on here?
- **Payoff:** Land the insight.
- **Close:** One line that makes them think or feel. No forced CTAs.

### Long-Form Structure (BrayDonDon video essays)
- One big question per video
- Three braided threads: personal story + research + systems explanation
- Tone: honest, reflective, energetic — mini-doc or video essay, not tutorial
- Target length: 10–20 min with occasional deeper dives

### Channel North Star (BrayDonDon)
"This channel exists to figure out what it feels like to be young and trying to understand how the world actually works when the systems you live in keep changing the rules."

### Decision Filter (Run Before Any Video)
- Does this come from a real feeling, memory, or pattern I actually care about?
- Does it help explain how the world works in a way the audience can feel?
- Can I make this without a huge production burden?
- Does it fit the channel thesis or am I chasing the algorithm?

## 🔁 Clipping / Systematic Income Workflow

### Copyright Safety — What Is Safe to Use
- **Podcasts with explicit clip permissions** — many large shows (Lex Fridman, Huberman Lab, etc.) allow fan clip channels; check description or website for license language
- **Creative Commons YouTube videos** — use the CC filter in YouTube search
- **Public domain content** — archive.org, U.S. government footage, pre-1928 material
- **Own long-form content** — best long-term source; zero copyright risk, cross-promotes the main channel
- **Legal protection is transformative use** — a clip must add something (captions, title card, editorial angle, framing). Pure reposts get struck. Editorially framed clips are legally much safer.
- **What gets struck:** Mainstream media, news networks, sports highlights without license, background music not cleared

### Finding Good Clips Without Watching Hours of Content

**The Transcript Method (most important free technique):**
Every YouTube video has an auto-generated transcript. Click "..." under any video → Open Transcript. Skim it like a document. Look for:
- Bold or surprising claims
- Moments where the speaker contradicts conventional wisdom
- Emotional shifts or confessions
- Specific stats or stories that feel quotable

This turns a 2-hour podcast into a 10-minute skim.

**The ChatGPT + Transcript Combo:**
Copy the raw transcript text → paste into ChatGPT free tier → prompt:
"Find the 5 most surprising, emotionally charged, or insight-dense moments in this transcript."
Identifies clip candidates in 30 seconds vs. 2 hours of listening. This is the real unlock.

### What Makes a Good Clip (Pattern to Internalize)
1. Something is at stake in the first 5 seconds — bold claim, surprising stat, tension
2. It resolves or escalates within 60 seconds — viewer gets payoff
3. It makes you want to tell someone — the "did you hear what they said about X" test

**Training method:** Search YouTube for "[podcast name] clips" and study what performs. Look at view counts, read comments, notice what the editor chose to start and end on. This is the same instinct that makes long-form videos work — just applied faster and at higher volume.

## 💡 Key Mindset Shifts Logged in This Thread

- **"Passive income" is a myth for YouTube.** Reframe as *systematic income* — the system reduces friction, but Brayden's judgment and effort are always in the loop.
- **AI is a speed and assist layer, not a replacement.** Fully automated channels are oversold and often demonetized. Realistic model: AI handles research, transcription, drafts, clip identification; Brayden handles judgment, selection, edit decisions, publishing.
- **False optimism is a calibration risk.** Most "make money with AI YouTube" content is shovel-selling. The people profiting often teach the system rather than run it. Return to this framing whenever a new AI income promise looks tempting.
- **Volume beats perfection early.** A messy clip that goes up beats a perfect one still in the editing queue.
- **Two tracks, one skill-building engine.** The deliberate choice to let Track A (clipping) and Track B (BrayDonDon) share skills is the correct strategic framing. Not two separate burdens — one compounding practice.
